Distributor integrations live on the **Distributors** tab of the Integrations page (**Integrations → Distributors**, [`/integrations#distributors`](/integrations#distributors)). They let you bring vendor licenses and live product pricing into Breeze: **Pax8** syncs client companies and license subscriptions you can map to organizations and link to recurring contract lines, and **TD SYNNEX Pricing** (EC Express) looks up real-time cost, MSRP, and stock availability you can import straight into your [Product Catalog](/features/product-catalog/).

The tab has two sub-tabs:

- **Pax8** — [`/integrations#pax8`](/integrations#pax8)
- **TD SYNNEX Pricing** — [`/integrations#tdsynnex-ec`](/integrations#tdsynnex-ec)

<Aside type="note">
  Distributor integrations are **partner-account only**. They are configured once for your MSP and shared across all of your customers. If you sign in with an organization-scoped account, both sub-tabs show a message that the integration is available to partner accounts only — there is nothing to configure at the organization level.
</Aside>

<Aside>
  Legacy note: an older TD SYNNEX **Digital Bridge** connector is intentionally hidden. Its catalog endpoint isn't entitled for pricing data, so **TD SYNNEX Pricing** (EC Express) is the working connector documented here. If you're looking for TD SYNNEX product pricing, use the **TD SYNNEX Pricing** sub-tab.
</Aside>

## Connecting a distributor

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Pax8">

Pax8 connects with your Pax8 **OAuth client credentials**. Once connected, Breeze pulls your Pax8 companies and their license subscriptions so you can map them to Breeze organizations and link them to recurring contract lines.

<Steps>
1. Open **Integrations → Distributors → Pax8** ([`/integrations#pax8`](/integrations#pax8)).

2. In the **Connection** card, fill in:
   - **Display name** — a label for this connection (defaults to `Pax8`).
   - **Client ID** — your Pax8 OAuth client ID.
   - **Client secret** — your Pax8 OAuth client secret.
   - **Webhook secret** *(optional)* — stored with the integration, but not required by the ordering or scheduled-sync workflow.

3. Click **Connect Pax8**. Secrets are stored encrypted; once saved, each field shows a **Configured** badge. When updating an existing connection you can leave a secret field blank to keep the stored value.

4. Click **Test connection** to verify the credentials reach Pax8.

5. Click **Sync now** to pull companies and subscriptions. The sync runs in the background — the **Sync status** card shows the last sync time, status, and any last error once it finishes.
</Steps>

<Aside type="caution">
  Pax8 write actions (connecting, syncing, mapping companies, linking subscriptions, and authoring or submitting orders) are **MFA-gated** on the server. If you see an "MFA required" message, enable multi-factor authentication on your Breeze account and retry.
</Aside>

**What syncs from Pax8**

- **Companies** — your Pax8 client companies, with status, shown in the **Company mapping** table. Map each Pax8 company to a Breeze organization (or leave it unmapped).
- **License subscriptions** — pulled per company, including product name, vendor, quantity, unit cost, unit price, currency, billing term, and status.
- **Contract-line linkage** — once a company is mapped to an organization, each subscription can be linked to a manual contract line. Breeze keeps the billing quantity on that contract line and records the Pax8 quantity separately as an observation.

<Aside type="caution" title="Behavior change: Pax8 no longer overwrites billing quantities">
  Existing links with `sync_enabled` will **stop automatically updating contract-line quantities from Pax8**. This is intentional: the quantity reported by the Pax8 subscription API does not reliably match the quantity Pax8 invoices. The Breeze order ledger and its linked manual contract-line quantity are now the billing source of truth. Scheduled sync records Pax8's reported quantity for comparison and surfaces drift instead of silently changing customer billing.
</Aside>

**Subscription links on the Integrations page**

A subscription only becomes linkable after its company is mapped to a Breeze organization. On **Integrations → Distributors → Pax8**, each subscription row offers:

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Link for observation** | Connect the subscription to a contract line (or create a new manual line) |
| **Change link** | Re-point the subscription to a different contract line |
| **Pause observations** | Stop recording Pax8 quantity observations for this link while keeping the link intact |
| **Resume observations** | Resume recording observations and drift detection for this link |
| **Unlink** | Remove the connection between the subscription and the contract line |

These Integrations-page actions manage the contract-line link and whether scheduled sync records observations; that table's **Pax8 reported** column is not a Breeze billing quantity. To compare quantities or stage an order action, open the mapped organization's **Pax8** tab. The organization tab shows **Breeze quantity** (the authoritative manual contract-line billing quantity), **Pax8 reported** (the latest known observation), and a **Drift** badge when both values are known and disagree. A missing or unknown Pax8 quantity is not treated as zero and does not produce a drift warning.

### Prepare an organization for ordering

Ordering is managed from the organization's **Pax8** settings tab, but remains a partner-account operation:

1. Sync the integration so the Pax8 company, subscriptions, and product mappings are current.
2. Open **Settings → Organizations → _organization_ → Pax8**, or go directly to `/settings/organizations/<org-id>#pax8`.
3. Map exactly one Pax8 company to the organization.
4. Confirm the mapped company is **Active** in Pax8 and has primary **Admin**, **Billing**, and **Technical** contacts. The organization tab shows the Pax8 company status. When the mapping is not order-ready, it shows one combined warning; it does not show contact details or separate indicators for the three contact roles.
5. Link recurring subscriptions to the corresponding manual contract lines. A linked line is required before the organization tab can stage a quantity change or cancellation.

The **New order** action stays unavailable until the company mapping is order-ready and at least one active Product Catalog item is mapped to a Pax8 product.

### Build and submit a direct order

The organization tab keeps one mutable direct order per organization. Returning to **New order** reopens that draft until it is submitted.

<Steps>
1. Click **New order** and choose an active, Pax8-mapped catalog product.

2. Set its billing term and quantity. If Pax8 reports commitment choices, select one. Complete any product-specific provisioning fields that you know; Breeze leaves all of these fields optional because Pax8 does not identify required fields in its metadata.

3. Add the product to the order. You can edit its commitment and provisioning details or remove the line while the order is still mutable.

4. To change an existing subscription, enter the target **Breeze quantity** and click **Stage change**. To cancel, click **Cancel** and confirm the warning. Breeze checks the subscription's active commitment dependencies before staging either action and blocks increases, decreases, or early cancellation that Pax8 says are not allowed. If the active commitment cannot be determined unambiguously, refresh the Pax8 data and resolve that ambiguity before ordering.

5. Click **Review & submit**. Breeze first sends a mock Pax8 order for new-subscription lines. If Pax8 returns validation details, Breeze shows the raw message beside the affected line so you can add or correct provisioning values. Quantity changes and cancellations are dependency-checked when staged; Pax8's mock-order preflight applies only to new subscriptions.

6. After preflight passes, Breeze submits each action and records the result on every line. Successful quantity changes and cancellations update the linked manual contract-line quantity (`0` for a completed cancellation); successful new subscriptions update their linked line when one exists.
</Steps>

<Aside type="caution" title="Do not blindly retry an uncertain submission">
  Pax8 does not accept an idempotency key for these writes. If Breeze cannot determine whether a request completed, the line is marked **Needs reconcile** instead of being sent again. Click **Reconcile**: Breeze reads Pax8 orders and subscriptions and reclassifies the existing attempt without issuing another write. If the result remains unknown, investigate before taking any manual action in Pax8 or Breeze.
</Aside>

The order history shows direct and quote-originated orders with their current status. Selecting an order changes the URL hash to `#pax8/<order-id>`, so the same order can be bookmarked and browser Back/Forward navigation works.

### Complete an order staged from a quote

Accepting a quote that contains Pax8-backed catalog items creates the invoice and contracts as usual, then stages a Pax8 order in the **same database transaction**. Acceptance does **not** submit anything to Pax8 and a missing company mapping never blocks the customer from accepting.

On a converted quote, technicians see **“This quote staged a Pax8 order (N items) awaiting provisioning details”**. Follow **Open Pax8 order** to the organization's deep link, map an order-ready company if needed, then review each new-subscription line. The catalog item, quote source, and linked recurring contract line are preserved; you only complete the commitment and provisioning details. Run preflight and submit it using the same flow as a direct order. One-time quote lines can be ordered, but are not linked to a recurring contract line.

  <TabItem label="TD SYNNEX Pricing">

**TD SYNNEX Pricing** connects to TD SYNNEX **EC Express** to look up real-time cost, MSRP, and stock availability, and import products straight into your [Product Catalog](/features/product-catalog/). It authenticates with your EC Express account credentials.

<Steps>
1. Open **Integrations → Distributors → TD SYNNEX Pricing** ([`/integrations#tdsynnex-ec`](/integrations#tdsynnex-ec)).

2. Fill in your EC Express connection details:
   - **Customer No** — your TD SYNNEX customer number.
   - **Region** — your account region (defaults to `US`).
   - **Email** — your EC Express account email.
   - **Password** — your EC Express account password (stored masked).

3. Tick **Enabled**, then click **Save settings**. The password is stored securely and shown masked afterward.

4. Click **Test connection** to verify the credentials against EC Express. The status label shows whether the last test succeeded or failed (with the error message on failure). *Test connection requires the integration to be enabled.*
</Steps>

**What you can pull from TD SYNNEX**

TD SYNNEX Pricing is a **live lookup + import** flow rather than a background sync:

- **Product lookup** — search by SYNNEX SKU or manufacturer part number. Each result shows the product name, status, description, your **cost**, **MSRP**, **total available** quantity, and a per-warehouse stock breakdown (available, on order, ETA).
- **Import to catalog** — import a result into your Product Catalog. The **sell price** is pre-filled from your partner default markup over cost (falling back to MSRP, then cost) and is editable. A live margin summary is shown, warning you if the sell price is below cost.
- On import, Breeze records the product name, SKU (SYNNEX SKU or manufacturer part), description, your entered sell price, the cost basis, and whether the item is taxable (following your partner's hardware-tax policy).

<Aside>
  Lookups require the integration to be **enabled** and connected. For the full catalog-import workflow, see [Product Catalog → Importing from distributor catalogs](/features/product-catalog/#importing-from-distributor-catalogs).
</Aside>
